No credit goes unused.

A lifecycle system for StorySnap built around one problem: clients who use their credits repurchase. Clients who don't, churn. This system is the engine that changes that ratio — at scale, across 300 accounts, without flooding two Strategists with noise.

Each section of this document follows the same structure: a reusable AI skill was designed first — a structured prompt that encodes the problem, the constraints, the data sources, and the quality criteria. The skill was then run against the StorySnap brief to produce the output you see by default.

How do you slice 300 accounts so a machine can act on each one?

This skill instructs an LLM to build a segmentation model grounded in real data signals — not personas. Every segment must map to a concrete action: if it can't trigger something, it doesn't belong.

RULE No invented signals. Every input must be derivable from existing data sources today.

1. Pace Score

Pace Score = ( Credits Used / Credits Purchased ) ÷ ( Days Elapsed / 365 )

A score of 1.0 means the account is consuming credits exactly proportional to time elapsed. Above 1.0 = ahead of pace. Below 1.0 = behind.

Example: Month 6 of 12 (50% term elapsed), client used 35% of credits → 0.35 / 0.50 = 0.70 → Drifting.

Computed from Airtable: credits_purchased, credits_used, purchase_date. No external data needed.

I avoided weighting by deliverable type or recent velocity because the data to support it isn't reliably structured yet. The simple ratio is computable today and corrects as usage patterns emerge. Velocity trending is the next layer to add once the score is live.

2. Segment Definitions

Segment Qualifying Signal Primary Action Owner
New 0–30 days post-purchase Onboarding — set expectations, introduce portal, introduce all brands Automated
Accelerating Pace Score > 1.2 Cross-sell awareness + early renewal prep Automated
Healthy Pace Score 0.80–1.20, month 2+ Light nurture, cross-brand education, social proof Automated
Drifting Pace Score 0.50–0.79 Re-engagement nudges; Strategist notified but doesn't act yet Monitor
At Risk Pace Score < 0.50 after month 3 OR 2+ active flags Automated escalation + Strategist alert triggered Strategist
Expiration Watch < 60 days to term end AND > 20% credits remaining (any segment) Immediate Strategist escalation; automated urgency message Urgent
Expiration Watch is an override — it surfaces any account when the time window becomes critical, regardless of current segment. I considered splitting Drifting into Early/Late but that added a seventh segment without changing the action. The 0.50 threshold is where drift starts to compound: accounts below it rarely self-correct.

3. Flag System

Flags are account-level failure mode indicators. They stack freely on top of any segment.

Flag Detection Logic Data Source
behind_pace Pace Score < 0.80 for 2 consecutive weekly checks Airtable
single_service Only 1 unique deliverable type across all completed deliverables; no cross-brand history Airtable
testimonial_blocked Testimonial credits in purchase + no testimonial completed in 60+ days + Pace Score < 0.80 Airtable
lost_champion Primary contact departure detected AND replacement has < 2 email opens in last 30 days HubSpot + departure alert
dark No portal login in 30 days AND average open rate on last 4 sends < 20% Portal + HubSpot
dark requires two signals to avoid false positives from clients who don't open marketing email but are still active in the portal. testimonial_blocked is narrow by design — it only fires when there's evidence the client has testimonial credits and isn't using them, not just any slow account.

4. Priority Matrix

Segment 0 flags 1 flag 2+ flags
New Automated Automated Strategist watch
Accelerating Automated Automated Monitor
Healthy Automated Monitor Strategist watch
Drifting Automated Strategist watch Strategist alert
At Risk Strategist alert Strategist alert URGENT
Expiration Watch URGENT URGENT URGENT
At Risk always becomes a Strategist alert regardless of flag count because the compounding effect means waiting for a second flag is too late. Accelerating + flags is intentionally low urgency — a fast-spending account with a lost champion is concerning, but the primary problem is the departure, not the spend rate.

3. Talking Points: Lost Champion Outreach

Context: New contact may or may not know about the StorySnap relationship. Do not assume they do.

Opening — acknowledge the transition directly

"Hi [Name], I'm [Strategist] from [brand they purchased]. I wanted to reach out directly — I know transitions like this come with a lot on your plate, and I didn't want this account to fall through the cracks."

Orient them, don't pitch them

  • "Here's where things stand: [Company] has [X] credits available through [expiration date]. So far the team has produced [deliverable types] — I can send you links to everything that's been made."
  • "There's still [X] credits to work with, and enough time to produce [realistic options given timeline]."
  • Lead with "here's what exists and here's what's still possible." Not urgency.

The ask — low commitment

"Would you be open to a 20-minute call? I can walk you through what's been done, show you what's available, and we can figure out together whether there's anything useful to produce before the term ends."

If expiration is < 90 days

"Just worth knowing: the current bundle runs through [date]. Happy to talk through options — including whether it makes sense to roll anything forward."

If single_service flag is active

"One thing worth mentioning: [Company] has only used [brand/type] so far. The credits work across everything we make — including [other brand]. Happy to show you what else is available."

What to avoid

  • Do not reference the departed champion negatively
  • Do not open with credit urgency — it sounds like a collections call
  • Do not send a calendar link in the first message — it presumes too much
  • Do not CC or BCC the departed contact

Analytics & Rationale

What do you measure, what's broken before day one, and what do you build first?

This skill forces a builder's perspective: honest data gaps, leading (not lagging) metrics, a single critical path build for week one, and explicit reasoning for every scope cut. No filler — every sentence justifies a decision or surfaces a risk.

Inputs → Outputs

RULE Metrics must be specific and measurable ("utilization rate at month 6", not "engagement"). "Ran out of time" is not an acceptable scope cut reason.

1. North Star Metrics

55%+
Credit Utilization at Month 6
% of credits used by the halfway point, per cohort. Best clients repurchase at month 6–7 — this is the leading indicator, not GRR or NRR. Target: move from ~40% baseline to 55%+ by Q4 year one.
40%+
Drifting → Healthy Recovery Rate (Automated)
% of Drifting accounts that return to Healthy within 60 days without Strategist intervention. Holds the automated journey accountable. Below 30%: investigate sequence. Above 50%: the machine is working.
80%+
Strategist Alert-to-Action Rate
% of URGENT alerts that result in a logged HubSpot activity within 48h. Holds the alert design accountable — if Strategists aren't acting, the alerts are too frequent, too vague, or misprioritized.

2. Data & Tracking Gaps

Airtable ↔ HubSpot are not synced
Pace data lives in Airtable. Engagement and contact data live in HubSpot. No HubSpot workflow can enroll accounts based on pace today.
→ Map fields in week one. Start with Zapier as first layer. Scope native sync with product ops in parallel.
Pace Score doesn't exist as a field anywhere
The formula is computable from Airtable, but it's not a stored, queryable value. Without it, no segment enrollment can be automated.
→ Create formula field in Airtable first. Sync computed value to HubSpot account property every 24h via Zapier. This is the week-one build.
Portal login data is not in HubSpot
The dark flag requires portal engagement data. If login events aren't flowing into HubSpot, this flag can't be computed.
→ Confirm with product ops whether logins are tracked anywhere structured. If yes, pipe to HubSpot as last_portal_login. If not, add server-side event.
Champion departure is an alert, not a CRM field
If it arrives as an email notification rather than a HubSpot field update, it can't trigger automated workflows.
→ If email-only: create a manual Strategist process to update a HubSpot contact property (departed = true, departure_date). Build the workflow on top of that field. Works immediately, no dev work.
Cross-brand credit usage not rolled up at account level
To detect single_service, I need a count of distinct deliverable types per account. Airtable likely has this per-record, not rolled up.
→ Create a rollup field in Airtable counting distinct deliverable_type values per account. Sync count to HubSpot. Enables single_service detection and cross-sell targeting.

3. Week-One Build: Pace Score Sync

Week one · Critical path

Build Pace Score as a HubSpot account property

Everything — segments, journey enrollment, alerts, flags, two of three north star metrics — is built on top of pace. Nothing in the system can run without it.

What it unlocks immediately: the moment Pace Score exists as a HubSpot property, every segment enrollment criterion is implementable as a workflow filter. The Drifting journey can enroll accounts. The At Risk alert can fire. The priority matrix becomes operational.

How to build it in week one: create the formula field in Airtable, validate it against 5–10 known accounts manually, then set up a Zapier push to a HubSpot account property every 24 hours. Imperfect — but working and auditable on day seven.

4. Conscious Scope Cuts

✂
New account onboarding journey — step-by-step content
The onboarding journey is defined at the segment level but the content (what to say in week 1, how to introduce the portal, how to brief clients on all brands) deserves its own exercise. The core problem the brief identifies is underutilization mid-term, not early abandonment. Fixing drift and at-risk accounts recovers more revenue faster than optimizing the first 30 days.
✂
Accelerating account cross-sell journey
Accounts with Pace Score > 1.2 are a good problem. They're self-correcting. I defined the segment and noted the cross-sell opportunity, but didn't design the journey in detail because the ROI is lower than fixing accounts at risk of not repurchasing at all. When the core system is running, Accelerating accounts are the right next investment.
